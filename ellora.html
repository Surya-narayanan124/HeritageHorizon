Ellora Caves

The Ellora Caves, located in Maharashtra, India, are a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for their monumental caves built between the 6th and 10th centuries.

There are 34 caves featuring Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain temples and monasteries carved directly into the rock face.

The most spectacular is Cave 16, the Kailasa temple, which is the largest monolithic rock excavation in the world.

Ellora represents a harmonious coexistence of different faiths and an extraordinary achievement of Indian rock-cut architecture.
