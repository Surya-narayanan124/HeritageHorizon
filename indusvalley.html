Indus Valley Toy

The Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE) produced remarkable toys, including miniature carts and animal figurines, which showcase the craftsmanship of one of the world’s earliest urban cultures.

These toys, often made of terracotta, demonstrate not only the artistic sense but also insights into the daily life and technology of the time.

A famous example is the terracotta cart toy excavated at Harappa, which features wheels and a yoke for animals.

Such discoveries provide valuable information about trade, transport, and the lives of children thousands of years ago.
