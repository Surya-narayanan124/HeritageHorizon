The construction of the Taj Mahal spanned two decades, completed in 1648 for the central structure and later around 1653 for the adjoining buildings. Over 20,000 artisans, masons, stonemasons, and craftsmen from different parts of the subcontinent and abroad were involved. The principal architect is believed to be Ustad Ahmad Lahori, supported by several regional experts.

What makes the Taj Mahal extraordinary is not just its sheer beauty but its structural harmony, symmetry, and intricate detailing. The complex also includes a mosque to the west, a guest house (jawab) to the east, formal gardens (Charbagh layout), reflecting pools, and a red sandstone gateway on the southern side. The entire layout is arranged to emphasize symmetry and balance.
