Hampi Stone Chariot

The Hampi Stone Chariot is an iconic monolithic monument located within the Vittala Temple complex in Hampi, Karnataka, India. Hampi was once the capital of the powerful Vijayanagara Empire during the 14th‚Äì16th centuries. Over centuries, Hampi became a grand seat of power, art, commerce, and religion, and today its ruins are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The Stone Chariot is carved from granite and resembles a traditional temple chariot used during festivals. Its design includes richly carved wheels, pillars, and sculptures of deities, lotus motifs, and mythological scenes. It stands on a raised stone platform and appears to be drawn by stone wheels (though the wheels are fixed). It is believed to have been dedicated to Garuda, the vehicle (vahana) of Lord Vishnu.

Surrounding the chariot are numerous ruins of temples, mandapas, shrines, and a river valley landscape of boulder-strewn hills, which add to the dramatic beauty of the site. The architecture in Hampi reflects a blend of Dravidian, Vijayanagara, and Hindu sculptural traditions.
