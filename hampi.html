Hampi Chariot

The Stone Chariot of Hampi is one of the most iconic monuments of the UNESCO World Heritage Site at Hampi, Karnataka, India. It is located inside the Vittala Temple complex.

It resembles a temple chariot and is dedicated to Garuda, the carrier of Lord Vishnu. The chariot is actually a shrine with intricate carvings.

Built during the reign of King Krishnadevaraya of the Vijayanagara Empire, it represents the architectural brilliance of the period.

The chariot is a symbol of Karnataka tourism and is featured on Indian currency notes as well.
