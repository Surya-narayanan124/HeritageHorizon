India Gate

India Gate is a majestic war memorial located in New Delhi, India, designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and completed in 1931. It commemorates the approximately 82,000 soldiers of the undivided British Indian Army who died during World War I and the Afghan War of 1919.

The structure is built of yellow and red sandstone and stands about 42 meters tall. It resembles the Arc de Triomphe in Paris in its imposing arch design. Surrounding the base is Amar Jawan Jyoti, a continuously burning flame erected in 1971 after India’s victory in the Indo-Pak war.

Located in the heart of Delhi, India Gate marks the eastern end of the ceremonial Rajpath (now Kartavya Path). The area around it is popular for evening walks and national celebrations.

Over time, India Gate has become more than a memorial — it is also a focal point for public gatherings and a symbol of national pride.
